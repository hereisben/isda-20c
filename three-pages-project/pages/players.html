Since joining Manchester United, Bruno Fernandes has been the heart of the team’s midfield, dictating play with his vision, creativity, and leadership. His ability to find spaces and deliver precise passes makes him one of the most influential playmakers in the Premier League. Whether through assists or long-range strikes, Fernandes has been the driving force behind United’s attacking play.

This season, Fernandes has stepped up even further, often carrying the team in crucial matches. His recent performance against Liverpool, where he scored a stunning long-range goal and assisted Marcus Rashford, was a testament to his quality. Despite facing criticism at times for his risk-taking, his ambition and game-changing ability remain irreplaceable.

Beyond statistics, Fernandes is also a vocal leader, setting high standards for his teammates. With Erik ten Hag’s tactical system relying heavily on midfield dominance, Fernandes’ role in United’s success cannot be overstated. As the season progresses, United’s hopes of silverware will largely depend on their Portuguese maestro maintaining his top form.

Marcus Rashford’s Season So Far: Strengths & Areas for Improvement

After a breakout campaign last season, Marcus Rashford has faced a more challenging year in 2024/25. While he remains one of United’s most dynamic attacking threats, inconsistency in finishing and decision-making has drawn mixed reactions from fans. His ability to beat defenders with pace and skill is unmatched, but he has often struggled to convert clear-cut chances into goals.

One of Rashford’s biggest strengths is his versatility, being able to play across the front three. However, his most effective position remains on the left-wing, where he can cut inside and unleash powerful shots. His partnership with Rasmus Højlund is still developing, and fans are hopeful that the two can form a deadly attacking duo in the second half of the season.

Despite a slower start, Rashford has still delivered key performances, including a brace against Arsenal and a crucial winner against Newcastle. If he can improve his composure in front of goal and maintain fitness, he has the potential to hit double figures once again. With United pushing for a Champions League spot, Rashford’s contributions will be pivotal in the final stretch of the season.

Defensive Woes: How United Can Fix Their Backline Issues

Manchester United’s defensive struggles have been a major talking point this season, with injuries and inconsistent performances leading to dropped points. While Lisandro Martínez and Raphaël Varane have been solid when fit, their repeated absences have forced Erik ten Hag to rely on backup options. Harry Maguire and Victor Lindelöf have had mixed performances, leading to uncertainty at the back.

One of United’s biggest issues has been defending against set-pieces and counter-attacks. The high defensive line, while effective in possession-based play, has often been exploited by teams with pacey forwards. André Onana, despite making key saves, has struggled at times with ball distribution, which has contributed to defensive instability.

To address these problems, Ten Hag may look to reinforce the defense in the transfer window, with links to Jarrad Branthwaite and Jean-Clair Todibo. A more structured defensive approach, particularly against top opposition, could help reduce costly errors. If United can stabilize their backline, their top-four ambitions will look much more achievable.

The Erik ten Hag Effect: Has He Truly Improved Manchester United?

Since arriving at Old Trafford, Erik ten Hag has made significant progress, implementing a modern tactical approach while instilling discipline within the squad. His debut season saw United win the Carabao Cup, finish third in the Premier League, and secure Champions League football. Fans were hopeful that his second year would see further improvements.

However, the 2024/25 season has been a mixed bag. While United have played some brilliant football, inconsistencies in defense and finishing have resulted in unexpected losses. Injuries to key players, including Martínez and Casemiro, have made Ten Hag’s job even more challenging. Despite these setbacks, his tactical philosophy remains clear, with pressing, structured buildup, and fluid attacking movement at the heart of his approach.

The biggest question now is whether Ten Hag can lead United back to the top. If he can resolve the team’s defensive issues and bring in key reinforcements, there’s a strong belief that United can challenge for trophies again. While patience is required, there’s no doubt that Ten Hag has laid the foundation for future success.

Rising Star: Is Kobbie Mainoo the Future of United’s Midfield?

At just 18 years old, Kobbie Mainoo has emerged as one of Manchester United’s most exciting young talents. The academy graduate has impressed fans and coaches alike with his composure, tactical awareness, and ability to control the tempo of the game. His breakthrough performance against Wolves, where he dominated the midfield, showcased his immense potential.

What sets Mainoo apart is his calmness under pressure. Unlike many young midfielders, he rarely panics in tight spaces and is comfortable playing progressive passes. His partnership with Casemiro has given United more stability, allowing Bruno Fernandes to operate further up the pitch. Ten Hag has already placed trust in Mainoo, giving him significant game time in crucial fixtures.

While he still has areas to develop—such as physicality and defensive positioning—Mainoo’s rise has been a welcome boost for United. With the right mentorship and regular minutes, he could become a long-term successor to United’s midfield legends like Paul Scholes and Michael Carrick. The future looks bright for this young Red Devil.

Best Ever Captains: A Look at United’s Greatest Leaders

Manchester United has been blessed with legendary captains who have led the club to domestic and European glory. Roy Keane, known for his unmatched intensity and leadership, remains one of the most iconic skippers in football history. His ability to rally the team, even in the toughest moments, made him a defining figure in United’s dominant era under Sir Alex Ferguson.

Another unforgettable leader was Nemanja Vidić, whose defensive mastery and fearless style made United’s backline one of the strongest in Europe. Alongside Rio Ferdinand, he captained United to multiple Premier League titles and played a crucial role in the 2008 Champions League triumph. His commanding presence made Old Trafford a fortress for several years.

More recently, Wayne Rooney and Bruno Fernandes have led the team in different ways. While Rooney inspired with his performances and passion, Fernandes has brought technical leadership, influencing games with his creative play. With United currently in transition, finding a true leader like Keane or Vidić could be key to returning to title-winning form.
